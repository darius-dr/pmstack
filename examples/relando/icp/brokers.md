# ICP: Brokeriai / agentūros (B2B pro)

*Worked example produced by `/pm:audience brokers`.*

## Snapshot

Lithuanian NT brokers and small-agency owners (2–20 agent shops in Vilnius, Kaunas, Klaipėda). Time-poor, transaction-volume incentivized, allergic to liability. Already know the domain; they don't need an explainer of what a servitutas is — they need it surfaced fast for a specific parcel.

## Job-to-be-done

> When I'm preparing a property to list or showing it to a serious buyer, I want a credible, source-cited due-diligence report I can hand to my client without spending my own afternoon collecting it, so I can close faster and reduce the chance of a deal collapsing at the notary.

## Top 3 functional needs

1. **Per-object due diligence on demand**, with cited official sources the client can verify themselves.
2. **Deal-killer early warning** — catch encumbrances, planning constraints, or court cases BEFORE the buyer's lawyer does.
3. **Works across all three property types** — sklypas, namas, butas — without separate workflows.

## Top 3 emotional needs

1. **Look credible.** A broker who hands the buyer a cited report looks more like a professional than one with verbal assurances.
2. **Sleep at night.** Reduce post-close-out liability — "I gave you the public-source data; you signed knowing X" is a much better posture than "I didn't know about X either".
3. **Not be embarrassed by the buyer's lawyer.** Discover whatever the buyer's lawyer will discover, first.

## Banned framing for this segment

- **No basic explanations.** Brokers know the domain. Don't define "servitutas" to them.
- **No anxiety-bait.** Pros are busy, not anxious.
- **No "AI-powered" as a feature label.** It's a mechanism; pros care about the output's reliability, not the buzzword.
- **No "modernize your workflow" generic SaaS talk.** Brokers don't want to be modernized; they want their next deal to not blow up.
- **No quoting B2C prices.** Pricing for B2B is *"susisiekime"*, not €49.

## Tone + voice notes

- **Register:** peer-to-peer professional. Same level of formality a senior broker would use with another senior broker.
- **Vocabulary:** domain-fluent. Use RC terminology, paskirties grupė, BP/TPDR codes without defining them.
- **Sentence length:** short to medium. Pros skim; reward skimming with structure (bulleted differentiators, bolded keywords).
- **Pronouns:** plural / formal (*Jūs*) in initial copy, or 1st-person plural (*mes / Jūsų komandai*). Match the seriousness.
- **Confidence:** matter-of-fact. *"Štai ką ataskaita parodo. Štai ko ji nepakeičia."*
